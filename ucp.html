UCP Website Redesign

Role UX / UI Design, Web Strategy, User Research
Duration 10 Weeks · 2025
Tools Figma, Squarespace, Google Maps, Google Calendar

Context

The Urban Collaborative Project (UCP) is a nonprofit based in Southeastern San Diego that connects residents with resources in health, housing, transportation, and civic engagement. Their existing website was outdated, hard to navigate, and didn’t reflect the scale of their impact or their current programs.


Our ENG 100D team partnered with UCP to redesign their Squarespace site into a community-centered platform that is easier to maintain, tells clearer stories about their work, and invites residents, partners, and volunteers to get involved.

The Problem

Community members, especially Spanish speakers and new visitors, struggled to understand what UCP does, find events, or access resources from the old site. Important information was buried, links were broken, navigation was unclear, and staff had limited time and technical capacity to keep content updated.

The Solution

We redesigned the website around clear storytelling and simple navigation: a mission-forward homepage, an interactive projects map, a focus area “puzzle” interface, a Get Involved hub, and an events calendar. We also created editable templates and a technical handoff guide so staff can update pages and add new projects without needing a developer.




Design

Lo-Fi Prototypes

We began with hand-drawn sketches and low-fidelity wireframes for key pages: the homepage, a focus-area puzzle, the projects map, and a Get Involved page. These helped us test information architecture with stakeholders and align on what content needed to be surfaced for residents, volunteers, and partner organizations.




Design

Hi-Fi Prototypes

From there, we moved into high-fidelity designs in Figma and implementation in Squarespace. We leaned into UCP’s “putting the pieces together” motto with puzzle-inspired visuals, local photography, and warm colors that feel rooted in the neighborhood. Interactive map pins, cards, and buttons guide users toward events, resources, and ways to participate.



Redesign Highlights

  • Mission-forward homepage: clear statement of who UCP is, what they do, and featured stories that show their impact.


  • Focus Area puzzle: interactive tiles that visually connect UCP’s five main focus areas and link to deeper content.


  • Interactive project map: Google Maps–based view of ongoing and past projects, with color-coded pins and popups that explain each initiative.


  • Get Involved hub: a single page that routes people to volunteer, attend meetings, collaborate as partners, or explore career opportunities.


  • Events calendar: embedded Google Calendar for meetings and programs, so community members can add events directly to their own calendars.


  • Built for handoff: page templates, content guidelines, and a technical manual so UCP staff can maintain and expand the site over time.


Final Takeaways

This project showed me how web design for nonprofits is as much about sustainability and trust as it is about visuals.

Design with the community, not just for them

Interviews with community members, staff, and agency partners revealed very different needs—from storytelling and cultural representation to data, reports, and impact metrics. Balancing those perspectives created a more honest and effective site.

Sustainability is a design requirement

Because staff have limited time and technical support, modular components, editable templates, and clear documentation were just as important as the UI. A beautiful site that can’t be updated quickly becomes another problem.

Small accessibility moves add up

Clearer labels, simplified navigation, resource pages, and a structure that can later support Spanish translation all helped the site feel more welcoming and usable for diverse residents in Southeastern San Diego.
